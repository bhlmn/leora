Tips & Tricks

If you are determined to do it on your own and just need a little extra push, these Tips and Tricks may be all you need. Each of these build upon one another, so the top is a great place to start!

Rally the Troops!

When it comes to having a beautifully organized home it’s important to keep key players in mind. You can’t do this alone. Is everyone in the home willing to commit to lasting change? Modifying behavior and habits is very challenging. Creating systems that are easy for all house members to use is just the first step. Modified communication can aid in the progression towards building good habits.

Respect the Space

Our homes and belongings are investments. Hard earned time and money has gone into every aspect of your home. And each piece has a purpose it was designed for. When we respect our spaces and our belongings by using them for their intended purpose, we extend their value and usefulness. Using them improperly breaks them down faster, meaning it will need to be replaced or repaired sooner.

Habits and Routines

There are many different areas that people struggle with in their homes. One I have encountered a lot is an untidy living space. If the first thing you see when you walk into your living room is things all over the floor, I might be able to help. I recommend creating a quick tidy up routine. This often means having some empty decorative baskets handy on a shelf. It takes little effort to grab one and fill up before heading out the door in the morning or before heading to bed at the end of the day. Another favorite of mine is finding a home for everything. Wouldn’t it be nice to always find what you are looking for? It is easier to find what you need when everything has a home. Of course, the key is to put it away after use. The last one I will share here is, let it go. When you find that you pick up the same broken toy that has not been played with but has merely been dumped out onto the floor for the third time today, toss it. I have seen so much time and energy wasted on picking up the same piece of rubbish over and over again.

Chore Chart

Most people have tried a chore chart already. If you have and you did not see much success, now is a good time to give it another chance. In combination with the first three tips, charts can add structure and accountability to these developing habits. Now you have 1) the family on board, 2) a mindset of respect, and 3) simple routines with systems that work. Your family is now set up to succeed with a maintenance schedule. If just one task gets done a day then the weekend is not spent catching up on a week’s worth of house work. Instead everyone gets to enjoy quality time together.

Boundaries

This one can be touchy for some people and that is understandable. What works for some might not work for all. That said, I would like to share some of what I have observed and tried myself. I was a stay at home mom for four years with two boys, very close in age. Kids are often blamed for the state of a home. But kids can learn to respect boundaries that are consistent and well explained. Learning that there is a time and place for certain behaviors do not need to be reserved for public spaces, they can be used at home as well.

Get Creative

Be resourceful, work with what you’ve got, take chances and shake things up. And if needs be, let me be your DIY buddy! If you have something you don’t use anymore see if you can repurpose it. Many of my clients have felt such joy from taking on a project and truly surprising themselves by what they can create. These creations often become the center of attention and the first thing they show their friends after the redesign is complete.
